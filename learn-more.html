Sea Turtle

Information

Sea turtles are ancient marine reptiles that migrate long distances and help maintain healthy seagrass beds and coral reefs. There are seven species, including the green and leatherback turtles.

Problems

Human activities threaten sea turtles through habitat loss, pollution, and plastic waste, which harm nesting sites and feeding grounds. Bycatch in fishing nets and illegal hunting further endanger their populations.

Information

Jellyfish are ancient marine animals with soft, umbrella-shaped bodies and trailing tentacles. They thrive in oceans worldwide, feeding on plankton and small fish.

Problems

Human activities pose challenges for jellyfish. Pollution, like plastic waste, can harm them, while overfishing disrupts marine food webs and may lead to jellyfish blooms. Climate change is also altering ocean conditions, affecting their habitats detrimentally.

Shark

Information

Sharks are marine predators essential for maintaining healthy ocean ecosystems. With over 500 species, they range from tiny dwarf lantern sharks to massive whale sharks.

Problems

Unfortunately, sharks face major threats from human activities. Overfishing, particularly for shark fins, has drastically reduced their populations. Bycatch, habitat loss, and pollution further endanger them, while climate change disrupts their food sources and migration.

Information

Whales are large, intelligent marine mammals that play a key role in ocean ecosystems by regulating the food chain and cycling nutrients. They range from the massive blue whale to smaller species like belugas.

Problems

Human activities threaten whales through ship collisions, noise pollution, and fishing gear entanglements. Although commercial whaling has decreased, it still impacts some species. Climate change and pollution also disrupt their food sources and migration patterns.
