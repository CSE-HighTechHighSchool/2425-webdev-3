Shark

Information

Sharks are marine predators essential for maintaining healthy ocean ecosystems. With over 500 species, they range from tiny dwarf lantern sharks to massive whale sharks.

Problems

Unfortunately, sharks face major threats from human activities. Overfishing, particularly for shark fins, has drastically reduced their populations. Bycatch, habitat loss, and pollution further endanger them, while climate change disrupts their food sources and migration.

Information

Jellyfish are ancient marine animals with soft, umbrella-shaped bodies and trailing tentacles. They thrive in oceans worldwide, feeding on plankton and small fish.

Problems

Human activities pose challenges for jellyfish. Pollution, like plastic waste, can harm them, while overfishing disrupts marine food webs and may lead to jellyfish blooms. Climate change is also altering ocean conditions, affecting their habitats and encouraging the spread of some species.
